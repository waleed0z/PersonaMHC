Anxiety
▼

Anxiety

Anxiety is your brain’s way of reacting to stress and alerting you of potential danger. It's normal in many situations — but if it becomes overwhelming or persistent, it may be an anxiety disorder.

Symptoms:

  • Nausea, dizziness, trembling
  • Fatigue, irritability, muscle aches
  • Restlessness, fast heart rate, difficulty breathing

Types of Anxiety Disorders:

  • GAD: Constant worrying, worst-case scenarios
  • OCD: Recurring thoughts + compulsive actions
  • PTSD: Triggered by trauma; flashbacks & nightmares
  • Panic Disorder: Sudden intense fear and chest pain
  • SAD: Social fear of judgment or embarrassment
  • Phobias: Intense fear of specific things/situations

Treatment & Coping:

  • Healthy lifestyle: diet, sleep, exercise
  • Therapy: CBT, talk therapy
  • Meditation & mindfulness
  • Medication if necessary (consult doctor)
  • Talking to others, support systems

More tips: How to Fight Back When Your Anxiety Attacks

Depression
▼

Depression

Depression is a mood disorder that causes persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest. It affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities.

Symptoms:

  • Persistent sadness, hopelessness
  • Loss of interest or pleasure
  • Fatigue, sleep changes, appetite changes
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

Treatment & Coping:

  • Therapy (CBT, talk therapy)
  • Medication (antidepressants)
  • Exercise, healthy lifestyle
  • Support from friends/family

More info: NIMH: Depression

Bipolar Disorder
▼

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder causes extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression).

Symptoms:

  • Manic episodes: high energy, reduced need for sleep, risky behavior
  • Depressive episodes: low energy, sadness, hopelessness
  • Rapid mood changes

Treatment & Coping:

  • Mood stabilizers, medication
  • Therapy
  • Routine and support

More info: NIMH: Bipolar Disorder

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
▼

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

OCD is characterized by unwanted and intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors or mental acts (compulsions).

Symptoms:

  • Obsessions: fear of germs, unwanted thoughts
  • Compulsions: excessive cleaning, checking, counting
  • Distress if rituals are not performed

Treatment & Coping:

  • Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Medication (SSRIs)
  • Exposure and response prevention

More info: IOCDF: About OCD

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
▼

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. It causes intense, disturbing thoughts and feelings related to the experience.

Symptoms:

  • Flashbacks, nightmares
  • Severe anxiety, avoidance
  • Emotional numbness
  • Hypervigilance

Treatment & Coping:

  • Therapy (CBT, EMDR)
  • Medication
  • Support groups

More info: VA: PTSD

Schizophrenia
▼

Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia is a serious mental disorder in which people interpret reality abnormally. It may result in hallucinations, delusions, and disordered thinking.

Symptoms:

  • Hallucinations, delusions
  • Disorganized speech and behavior
  • Social withdrawal
  • Lack of motivation

Treatment & Coping:

  • Antipsychotic medication
  • Therapy
  • Community support

More info: NIMH: Schizophrenia

ADHD
▼

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder marked by patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with functioning or development.

Symptoms:

  • Difficulty focusing
  • Impulsivity
  • Hyperactivity
  • Forgetfulness

Treatment & Coping:

  • Behavioral therapy
  • Medication
  • Organizational skills training

More info: CDC: ADHD

Eating Disorders
▼

Eating Disorders

Eating disorders are serious conditions related to persistent eating behaviors that negatively impact health, emotions, and daily life.

Types:

  • Anorexia nervosa
  • Bulimia nervosa
  • Binge-eating disorder

Symptoms:

  • Preoccupation with weight, food, or body shape
  • Extreme dietary restrictions or overeating
  • Distorted body image

Treatment & Coping:

  • Therapy (CBT, family-based)
  • Medical monitoring
  • Nutritional counseling

More info: NIMH: Eating Disorders

Panic Disorder
▼

Panic Disorder

Panic disorder is characterized by recurrent, unexpected panic attacks—sudden periods of intense fear that may include palpitations, sweating, and shortness of breath.

Symptoms:

  • Sudden and repeated panic attacks
  • Intense fear of losing control
  • Avoidance of places where attacks have occurred

Treatment & Coping:

  • Therapy (CBT)
  • Medication
  • Relaxation techniques

More info: NIMH: Panic Disorder

Social Anxiety Disorder
▼

Social Anxiety Disorder

Social anxiety disorder is an intense, persistent fear of being watched and judged by others. It can affect work, school, and other daily activities.

Symptoms:

  • Fear of social situations
  • Worry about embarrassment or humiliation
  • Avoidance of social interactions
  • Physical symptoms (blushing, sweating, trembling)

Treatment & Coping:

  • Therapy (CBT, exposure therapy)
  • Medication
  • Social skills training

More info: NIMH: Social Anxiety Disorder
